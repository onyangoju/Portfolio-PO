About

Pauline Onyango is a dynamic and multifaceted individual who thrives at the intersection of art, science, and social justice. She is currently pursuing her undergraduate studies in IT at Maseno University, navigating the fast-paced world of technology while staying abreast of the latest trends and innovations. Beyond her technical studies, Pauline is deeply committed to activism and feminism. As a poet, she channels her creativity into weaving words that capture personal and collective experiences. Her role as a research assistant allows her to merge analytical skills with a passion for addressing pressing societal issues. She also finds inspiration in philosophy and history, exploring the depths of human thought and experience. Her ability to connect with people on a meaningful level fuels her drive to create positive change in the community. Pauline is a certified Research Fellow for the Shamiri Intervention wellness program under Women Volunteers for Peace (WOVOP). She also gained valuable industry experience through an internship at the KIRDI Western Region Centre (KWRC).
